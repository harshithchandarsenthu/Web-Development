Subject Descriptions

History
History is the study of past events, people, and civilizations. It helps us understand how societies changed over time and how important events shaped the modern world. Historians use sources such as documents, artifacts, photographs, and eyewitness accounts to learn about the past. History includes topics like ancient civilizations, wars, discoveries, rulers, revolutions, and cultural developments. By studying history, people can learn from past successes and mistakes, understand different cultures, and develop critical thinking skills.
Geography
Geography is the study of the Earth, its features, environments, and the people who live on it. It explores topics such as landforms, climates, natural resources, populations, and cultures around the world. Geography is divided into physical geography, which studies natural features like mountains and rivers, and human geography, which focuses on human activities and settlements. By learning geography, people can better understand how humans interact with the environment and how different regions of the world are connected.
